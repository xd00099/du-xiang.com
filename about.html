My Journey to Data Science

#learning #love #datascience #computerscience

Passions don't start with nothing.

During my first year as an undergraduate studying data science, I was not determined. Bewildered in this new field, I could hardly explain my major to strangers about what I was studying. We all understood what data was, but data science, the study of data, well, I was not convinced for the need for such a rising major dedicated to data.

My academic research started with my work in brain and memory. Dealing with brain data was not easy. The data I worked with always had a lot of missingness as those data were collected from a memory-compromised group. Data science knowledge really helped me pinpoint what statistical methods to be used to fill in the missingness. In addition, the raw MRI data requires a lot of preprocessing before it can be used for statistical analysis. Gradually developing the knowledge of data science ETL (Extract, transform, load) process, I was able to pipeline the preprocessing on the server so that everything can be done automatically. This was the first time I saw data/computer science in action that boosted my work efficiency.

After applying data science in the neurological field, I had an opportunity to research in the Natural Language Processing field. The processing of natural language, drawing knowledge out of a large amount of language data, and making inferences about them, this whole process, teaching the computer to learn human languages, started me into the field of machine learning. After doing my work on Topic Modeling, I was able to highlight novel topic clusters from publications that were previously deemed unrelated. As a result, ethics could be taken into greater considerations when practicing research. I, for the second time, realized how much data science could aid the practical use of data, and I am voracious about learning more and more.
